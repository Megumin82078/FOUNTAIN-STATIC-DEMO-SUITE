What is the Problem?

Transferring medical records in Canada can take up to 30 business days and cost at least $30 per transfer. Only 29% of physicians report sharing health data outside their practice, yet they needed external information for about half (52%) of their patients in the past 12 months. Because of limited connectivity between clinics, hospitals, and different jurisdictions—and insufficient data privacy legislation—patients visiting multiple specialists often face duplicate tests and uninformed treatments due to poor data sharing. These inefficiencies delay care, drive up overall costs, and leave patients, providers and governments frustrated.

Our Mission

At Fountain, we believe every patient deserves full control over their health data. By integrating advanced artificial intelligence that provides intensive support directly to doctors with robust encryption, we break down the barriers between clinics, hospitals, and jurisdictions. Our solution ensures that vital information is not only securely accessible and efficiently shared but also empowers healthcare professionals with real-time insights for better decision-making.

We eliminate redundant processes, foster transparent collaboration, and unify the healthcare ecosystem so that patients receive timely, informed care while doctors benefit from AI-driven decision support. Our ultimate goal is to build a trust-driven network where data is used and shared responsibly and effectively to transform healthcare for everyone.

“As referenced in your proposal [Fountain Proposal], data fragmentation and lack of standardization hinders the secure access, use and exchange of health information across digitally connected health systems in Canada.” – Elizabeth Toller, Director General, Health Care Strategies, Health Canada.
